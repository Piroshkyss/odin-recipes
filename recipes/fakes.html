Lentil soup recipe

Description

Fakes (Greek: Φακές, pronounced fah-KESS) is a classic Greek lentil soup known for its hearty, comforting, and nutritious qualities. This rustic dish features lentils simmered with vegetables like onion, carrot, and celery in a tomato-based broth, seasoned with bay leaves, herbs, and olive oil. Fakes is a staple in Greek households, especially during colder months or fasting periods, and is often served with a drizzle of olive oil, a splash of vinegar, and fresh bread

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups green or brown lentils
  • 6 cups water or vegetable stock
  • 1 red or yellow onion, chopped
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, sliced or minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1–2 bay leaves
  • 1–2 cups canned crushed tomatoes or tomato puree
  • ¼–½ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano (optional)
  • Fresh herbs (parsley, thyme, or mint), chopped (optional)
  • Red wine vinegar, for serving (optional)
  • Chili flakes, for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. Sort through the lentils to remove any debris, then rinse well. Optionally, soak them in lukewarm water for 1 hour and drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced carrots and celery. Sauté for another 3–5 minutes until slightly softened.
  4. Stir in the lentils, crushed tomatoes or tomato puree, bay leaves, and water or vegetable stock. Bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 45–60 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add more water if needed to reach your desired soup consistency.
  6. Season with salt, black pepper, and oregano (if using). Simmer until the lentils are tender and the flavors meld.
  7. Remove the bay leaves. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  8. Serve hot, drizzled with extra olive oil and a splash of red wine vinegar. Garnish with fresh herbs and chili flakes if desired. Enjoy with crusty bread.
